GDP & FDI

India's GDP is projected to grow between 6.5% to 7% in 2024-25. The economy showed resilience with an 8.2% growth in FY 2024, exceeding 8% in three out of four quarters. India ranks as the fifth-largest economy globally in 2024, with a GDP of $3.94 trillion. The services sector remains the largest contributor to GDP at 54.7%, followed by industry at 27.6% and agriculture at 17.7%. India continues to attract significant foreign direct investment (FDI), with inflows expected to increase due to government initiatives to liberalize FDI policies and improve ease of doing business.

Make-In-India

The Indian stock market indices, such as the Sensex and Nifty 50, have shown strong performance, with the Sensex posting a 3.17% gain in September 2024. Positive investor sentiment has been bolstered by the government's focus on enhancing domestic production capabilities and attracting foreign investments. Despite overall positive trends, the stock market has experienced some volatility, particularly around key economic announcements and global economic conditions.

Capital Market

India’s capital market in 2024 is poised for growth, driven by expanding retail participation, increased Foreign Portfolio Investments (FPIs), and a strong push towards ESG (Environmental, Social, Governance) investments. Digital platforms and AI integration are making trading more accessible and data-driven, enhancing market dynamics. Regulatory reforms by SEBI aim to improve transparency and investor protection, building confidence in the market. A focus on domestic manufacturing and infrastructure under initiatives like "Make in India" supports long-term growth. Sustainable sectors, such as renewable energy, are drawing significant investor interest, marking a shift toward greener investments. Overall, India’s capital market landscape is evolving with promising momentum and stability.

Economy

India's economy in 2024 is projected to grow around 7%, driven by strong domestic demand, manufacturing, and services. Inflation control remains a focus, with the RBI targeting 4%. Exports are expected to rise, while government reforms and investment initiatives aim to attract more foreign and domestic investments.

Political

In 2024, Prime Minister Narendra Modi's leadership will be crucial as India approaches general elections. He will leverage achievements in economic growth, infrastructure, and digital initiatives to galvanize voter support. Modi is likely to strengthen coalitions with regional parties to counter opposition and ensure political stability. His focus on continuity in reforms and welfare programs will aim to project a commitment to development. Intensified public engagement, especially with the youth, will be key to maintaining his connection with voters. Additionally, his proactive foreign policy will position India as a significant global player amid ongoing geopolitical challenges.
